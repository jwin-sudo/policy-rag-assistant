# Vendor Risk Management Procedure

Document Owner: Procurement and Security
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: Third-party vendors, service providers, and contractors

## 1. Purpose
This procedure governs vendor onboarding, risk assessment, contracting, and periodic reassessment.

## 2. Vendor Tiers
Vendors are tiered by data access and business criticality.
- Tier 1: Access to Restricted data or critical operations
- Tier 2: Access to Confidential data or non-critical operations
- Tier 3: Minimal data access and low business impact

## 3. Intake and Classification
Business sponsor submits vendor request with service description, data access profile, and expected contract value.

## 4. Due Diligence Requirements
### Tier 1
- Security questionnaire and evidence review
- Privacy and legal review
- Business continuity assessment
- Penetration test or equivalent assurance artifacts

### Tier 2
- Security questionnaire
- Privacy and legal review as needed
- SLA and support capability validation

### Tier 3
- Basic procurement checks and sanctions screening

## 5. Contract Controls
Contracts must include where applicable:
- Data processing terms
- Security obligations
- Breach notification timelines
- Audit rights
- Subprocessor disclosure obligations
- Data deletion commitments at termination

## 6. Risk Acceptance
High residual risk requires approval from risk owner and Security leadership before purchase or renewal.

## 7. Onboarding
No production access is granted until contractual and technical controls are complete.

## 8. Ongoing Monitoring
- Tier 1 vendors reviewed annually
- Tier 2 vendors reviewed every 18 months
- Tier 3 vendors reviewed at renewal

Triggers for immediate reassessment include incidents, ownership changes, or significant control failures.

## 9. Incident Coordination
Vendor incidents affecting company data or operations must be escalated through internal incident management processes.

## 10. Offboarding
At end of contract, vendor access is revoked and data return/deletion evidence must be collected and retained.

## 11. Documentation
Maintain records of assessments, approvals, exceptions, and remediation plans in procurement systems.

## 12. Metrics
Quarterly reporting includes:
- Number of active vendors by tier
- Assessment completion rates
- Overdue remediation items
- Vendors under risk exception

## 13. Exceptions
Exceptions require documented risk rationale, compensating controls, and expiration date.
