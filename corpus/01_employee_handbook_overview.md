# Employee Handbook Overview

Document Owner: People Operations
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: All employees, interns, and long-term contractors

## 1. Purpose
This handbook defines core workplace expectations at Northstar Analytics. It explains how teams collaborate, how performance is measured, and how employees can access support. This document is an entry point for policy interpretation and does not replace local legal requirements.

## 2. Company Values
- Act with integrity in every interaction.
- Prioritize customer trust and safety.
- Communicate clearly and respectfully.
- Take ownership of outcomes, not just tasks.
- Build inclusive teams where all voices are heard.

## 3. Employment Classifications
Employees are classified as full-time, part-time, temporary, or intern. Classification affects benefits eligibility, paid leave, and overtime treatment. HR maintains classification records and resolves disputes.

## 4. Work Hours and Scheduling
Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time. Outside this period, teams may set flexible schedules with manager approval. Employees must:
- Maintain an up-to-date working calendar.
- Attend required meetings unless excused.
- Respond to urgent internal messages within one business hour during scheduled work time.

## 5. Attendance and Leave
Employees must report planned and unplanned absences in the HRIS portal. Leave categories include:
- Vacation
- Sick leave
- Family and caregiver leave
- Public holiday leave
- Jury or civic duty leave

Managers approve leave based on team coverage and business needs. Retaliation for lawful leave usage is prohibited.

## 6. Compensation and Payroll
Payroll is processed semi-monthly. Employees must verify personal and banking details in HRIS. Any compensation discrepancy should be reported within five business days of payment. Unauthorized wage deductions are prohibited.

## 7. Benefits
Eligible employees receive medical, dental, and vision coverage, life insurance, and retirement plan access. Eligibility windows, enrollment events, and plan changes are administered by People Operations during onboarding and open enrollment.

## 8. Performance Management
Performance is reviewed twice per year using role-based expectations. Review inputs may include:
- Goal completion metrics
- Peer and cross-functional feedback
- Quality, reliability, and ownership indicators
- Development progress

Employees and managers are expected to document goals quarterly.

## 9. Learning and Development
Every employee receives an annual learning budget and is encouraged to complete at least one skills objective per quarter. Manager approval is required for external training over a defined spending threshold.

## 10. Workplace Conduct
All team members must comply with the Code of Conduct, anti-harassment rules, and security procedures. Violations may result in coaching, formal warning, suspension, or termination depending on severity.

## 11. Health and Safety
Employees must follow local safety protocols and report hazards immediately. Incident reports are submitted within 24 hours. For remote workers, the home office must meet minimum ergonomic and electrical safety expectations.

## 12. Equipment and Acceptable Use
Company-provided devices are for business use. Limited personal use is permitted if it does not:
- Interfere with work
- Violate law or policy
- Create security risk

Software installation requires IT approval.

## 13. Confidentiality
Employees must protect confidential business and customer information during and after employment. Sharing confidential information outside authorized channels is prohibited.

## 14. Separation and Offboarding
Upon separation, employees must return all company assets by the final working day unless alternate arrangements are approved. Access revocation is coordinated by IT and HR on the termination date.

## 15. Policy Exceptions
Any exception to this handbook requires written approval from People Operations and Legal. Temporary exceptions must include an expiration date and risk mitigation steps.
