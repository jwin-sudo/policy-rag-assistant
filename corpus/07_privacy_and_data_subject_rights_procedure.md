# Privacy and Data Subject Rights Procedure

Document Owner: Privacy Office
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: Processing of personal data for customers, users, and employees

## 1. Purpose
This procedure explains how Northstar Analytics handles privacy requests and obligations under applicable data protection laws.

## 2. Definitions
- Personal Data: information that identifies or can identify an individual.
- Data Subject Request (DSR): request to access, correct, delete, or restrict personal data processing.
- Controller/Processor: roles defined by applicable privacy law and contractual context.

## 3. Lawful Basis and Notice
Teams must identify a lawful basis before processing personal data and ensure privacy notice coverage is accurate, clear, and current.

## 4. Intake Channels
DSRs may be submitted via:
- Privacy request form
- Support email alias
- Customer success channel
- Mail or legal notice

All requests are logged in the privacy ticketing system.

## 5. Verification
Identity verification is required before releasing or deleting personal data. Verification requirements scale with sensitivity and risk.

## 6. Request Types
### Access
Provide copy of personal data and relevant processing details.

### Correction
Update inaccurate or incomplete information.

### Deletion
Delete data unless exemptions apply, such as legal obligations or security needs.

### Restriction or Objection
Limit processing where required by law.

### Portability
Provide machine-readable export when legally required and technically feasible.

## 7. Timelines
Standard response target is 30 days from verification, subject to lawful extension where complexity is high. Extensions must be communicated with reason.

## 8. Internal Fulfillment Workflow
- Privacy team triages request scope.
- Data owners gather records from systems of record.
- Legal validates exemptions and response language.
- Privacy team issues response and records closure evidence.

## 9. Exemptions and Denials
Any denial must cite legal basis and provide appeal path where applicable.

## 10. Cross-Border Transfers
Personal data transfers must use approved safeguards and contractual protections.

## 11. Data Breach Coordination
Privacy incidents are handled with Security under the Incident Response Playbook. Notification decisions follow legal and regulatory thresholds.

## 12. Vendor Coordination
When vendors process relevant personal data, vendor managers must coordinate fulfillment support and ensure contractual obligations are met.

## 13. Recordkeeping
For each DSR, maintain:
- Request type and date
- Verification steps
- Systems queried
- Decision and legal rationale
- Response date and content summary

Records are retained according to legal and retention requirements.

## 14. Training
Employees in support, product, legal, and security functions complete annual privacy training with scenario-based refreshers.

## 15. Quality Assurance
Privacy Office performs quarterly QA sampling to validate timeliness, accuracy, and consistency of request handling.

## 16. Exceptions
Procedure exceptions require Privacy Counsel approval and documented risk treatment.
