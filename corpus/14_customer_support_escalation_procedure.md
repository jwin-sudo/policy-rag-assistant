# Customer Support Escalation Procedure

Document Owner: Customer Operations
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: Support agents, technical support, customer success, engineering

## 1. Purpose
This procedure defines consistent triage and escalation for customer issues to improve resolution speed and communication quality.

## 2. Severity Definitions
- P1: Service unavailable or severe customer impact, no workaround
- P2: Major functionality degraded, workaround limited
- P3: Partial degradation, workaround available
- P4: Minor issue, informational request, or enhancement question

## 3. Intake Requirements
Every ticket must include:
- Customer account and contact
- Environment and affected feature
- Steps to reproduce
- Business impact statement
- Screenshots or logs when available

## 4. Initial Response Targets
- P1: 15 minutes
- P2: 1 hour
- P3: 4 business hours
- P4: 1 business day

## 5. Escalation Paths
### Technical Escalation
Triggered when issue exceeds support runbook scope or indicates service defect.

### Incident Escalation
Triggered for multi-customer impact, security concern, or outage indicators.

### Executive Escalation
Triggered when contractual commitments, strategic accounts, or reputational risk is high.

## 6. Communication Cadence
- P1: updates every 30 minutes
- P2: updates every 2 hours
- P3/P4: updates at meaningful milestones

## 7. Ownership Model
A single ticket owner coordinates cross-functional updates and ensures timeline accuracy.

## 8. Handover Standards
When handing off between teams, include:
- Current hypothesis
- Completed diagnostics
- Pending actions
- Expected next update time

## 9. Root Cause and Follow-Up
For P1 and P2 issues, complete root cause summary and preventive action plan within five business days.

## 10. Customer Closure Criteria
Ticket may close when:
- Customer confirms resolution, or
- Agreed waiting period passes after solution delivery, and
- Internal validation confirms no ongoing impact

## 11. Quality Controls
Support leadership reviews random closed tickets weekly for quality, empathy, and process compliance.

## 12. Reporting Metrics
- First response time
- Time to resolution
- Reopen rate
- Escalation rate by severity
- Customer satisfaction score

## 13. Exceptions
Any SLA exception requires manager approval and customer communication.
