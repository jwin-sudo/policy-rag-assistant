# Code of Conduct and Ethics

Document Owner: Legal and Compliance
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: Employees, contractors, vendors on company premises or systems

## 1. Purpose
This Code of Conduct establishes mandatory ethical standards for behavior at Northstar Analytics. It ensures a workplace that is lawful, respectful, and accountable.

## 2. Scope
This code applies to:
- In-office and remote work settings
- Company-sponsored travel and events
- Digital communications and collaboration platforms
- Interactions with customers, partners, and regulators

## 3. Core Expectations
All personnel must:
- Act honestly and in good faith.
- Avoid conflicts between personal and company interests.
- Treat others with dignity and respect.
- Follow applicable laws, contracts, and internal controls.
- Raise concerns in a timely manner.

## 4. Respectful Workplace
Discrimination, harassment, bullying, retaliation, and intimidation are prohibited. Protected traits include age, race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, marital status, pregnancy status, veteran status, and other legally protected categories.

Examples of prohibited behavior include:
- Unwanted sexual comments, jokes, or advances
- Repeated offensive remarks based on protected traits
- Threats, humiliation, or deliberate exclusion from work activities
- Retaliation against a person who made a report in good faith

## 5. Conflicts of Interest
Personnel must disclose actual or potential conflicts to Compliance. Common examples:
- Financial interests in competitors or suppliers
- Hiring or supervising family members without disclosure
- Accepting outside employment that impairs performance

Disclosed conflicts are assessed and may be mitigated through recusal, reassignment, or written restrictions.

## 6. Gifts, Hospitality, and Anti-Bribery
Do not offer or accept gifts, travel, or entertainment intended to improperly influence decisions. Cash gifts are never allowed. Any gift above the local policy threshold requires disclosure and approval. Bribery, facilitation payments, and kickbacks are strictly prohibited.

## 7. Confidentiality and Data Handling
Employees must safeguard confidential business, customer, and employee data. Confidential information may only be used for authorized business purposes and shared on a need-to-know basis.

## 8. Communications and Public Statements
Only authorized spokespeople may make public statements on behalf of Northstar Analytics. Social media use must not disclose confidential information, misrepresent company positions, or create reputational harm.

## 9. Financial Integrity
Books, records, and reports must be complete and accurate. False entries, off-book arrangements, and deliberate misclassification of expenses are prohibited.

## 10. Reporting Concerns
Concerns may be reported to:
- Direct manager
- People Operations
- Legal and Compliance
- Anonymous ethics hotline

Good-faith reporting is protected. Retaliation is a policy violation.

## 11. Investigation and Enforcement
Reported concerns are reviewed promptly and confidentially to the greatest extent possible. Investigations follow principles of fairness, proportionality, and documented evidence. Confirmed violations may lead to:
- Coaching or training
- Written warning
- Loss of privileges
- Suspension
- Termination
- Referral to law enforcement where applicable

## 12. Training and Acknowledgment
All personnel must complete annual code training and sign acknowledgment of understanding. Failure to complete required training may result in access restrictions or disciplinary action.

## 13. Policy Exceptions
No exception may waive legal obligations. Any approved exception must be documented by Legal and Compliance with scope, rationale, controls, and end date.
