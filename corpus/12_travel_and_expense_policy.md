# Travel and Expense Policy

Document Owner: Finance Operations
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Review Cycle: Annual
Applies To: All employees submitting business expenses

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines reimbursable business expenses, approval requirements, and documentation standards.

## 2. General Principles
- Spend company funds responsibly.
- Choose reasonable and cost-effective options.
- Submit complete and accurate records.
- Follow approval limits and timelines.

## 3. Pre-Approval Requirements
Travel requires manager approval before booking unless emergency business need is documented. International travel may require additional Legal, Security, or HR approvals.

## 4. Allowed Expense Categories
- Air, rail, and ground transport for business travel
- Lodging within approved rate ranges
- Reasonable meal expenses while traveling
- Required visa and travel documentation fees
- Conference registration and approved training events

## 5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- Personal entertainment
- Alcohol beyond policy limits
- Fines, penalties, and traffic violations
- Personal upgrades without business justification
- Expenses lacking valid receipts where receipt is required

## 6. Receipt Requirements
Receipts are required for expenses above the local threshold. Expense reports should be submitted within 15 days of travel completion.

## 7. Corporate Card Use
Corporate cards are for approved business purchases only. Personal use is prohibited.

## 8. Per Diem and Regional Rules
Where per diem applies, employees must follow published rates and local legal requirements.

## 9. Currency and Exchange
Foreign expenses should be reported in original currency and converted by system exchange rates on submission date unless policy specifies otherwise.

## 10. Approval Workflow
- Employee submits expense report
- Manager reviews business purpose and reasonableness
- Finance audits for policy compliance

## 11. Audit and Compliance
Finance may request additional documentation. Repeated non-compliance may lead to reimbursement denial or disciplinary action.

## 12. Fraud and Misrepresentation
Intentional falsification of expenses is treated as misconduct and may result in termination and legal action.

## 13. Exceptions
Exceptions require documented business justification and approval from Finance leadership.
